Hi there! My name is Miriam. Welcome to my personal homepage.

I am an economics and computer science student, living in Munich and Liverpool. I'm mainly interested in Game Theory and Mechanism Design, Data Science, and Optimisation, but I can get excited about everything that takes interdisciplinary approaches to economics, computer sciences, and statistics. In order to combine my passions, I would like to deepen my knowledge in the field of Operations Research in my further studies.

About Me

I hold a Bachelor of Science in Economics from the University of Munich (LMU Munich). After attending compulsory modules in statistics and econometrics, I specialised in game theory and applied econometrics. I wrote my bachelor thesis on comparing the complexity of strategies by counting the number of states needed to model the strategies as finite automata. I am also aiming for a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science at the LMU Munich, which I expect to obtain in summer 2020. In the academic year 2018/2019 I studied at the University of Liverpool on a scholarship as a visiting student. There I attended advanced bachelor and master courses on Algorithmic Game Theory & Mechanism Design, Optimisation, Data Mining, and Neural Networks. This spring, I will be writing my bachelor's thesis on stackelberg routing in routing games and ε-approximate Nash equilibria.

Off campus, you'll probably see me hanging out in cafes with my friends, following current affairs, or riding my bicycle.


Additional modules information

Comparing modules from different universities with each other with regard to their equivalence can be quite the hussle. In order to simplify these troubles a little, the following is a list of courses that have given me particularly relevant knowledge and skills for my further studies, as well as an outline of their respective content.

Mathematical Analysis and Calculus
Set theory, Relations, Mappings, Functions, Induction, Real and Complex numbers, Sequences and Series, Limit theorems, Continuous and differentiable functions in one and many variables, Taylor expansion, Integration
Linear Algebra
Vectors, Matrices, Linear algebra in Rn, Determinants, Rank, Eigenvalues & Eigenvectors
Logic and Discrete Structures
Discrete Mathematics (modular arithmetic, partial orders, algebraic structures), Logic (propositional and predicate logic, resolution, Prenex normal form)
Lectures on Probability, Stochastics, and Statistics
Probability theory, random variables, distributions for discrete and continuous random variables, central limit theorem, independence of events, correlation, hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, maximum likelihood estimation
Lectures on Econometrics
Linear regression with one or multiple regressors, p-values and t-tests, confidence intervals, logit and probit models, instrumental variable estimation, regression discontinuity design, difference-in-difference, panel data
Lectures on Algorithmic Game Theory & Mechanism Design
Load Balancing & Congestion Games, PoA, PoS, Mechanisms with or without money, Combinatorial auctions, Sponsored search auctions
Optimisation
Linear Programs, Simplex algorithm, Duality, Maximum flow problems
Computational Intelligence
Evolutionary computation (Genetic Algorithms, Genetic Programming, Particle Swarm Optimisation), Neural Networks (Single & Multi-layer Perceptrons, Radial-basis Function Networks, Support Vector Machines, Self-Organising Maps)
Data Mining and Visualisation
Perceptron, classification algorithms, clustering algorithms, Natural Language Processing, Dimensionality reduction (PCA, SVD), Implementation of Perceptron and k-means clustering algorithm with Python
Introduction to Data Science
Metric geometry (point clouds, metric spaces, distance functions), clustering methods, computational geometry (Voronoi diagram, alpha-complexes), Graphs and Trees (Minimum spanning tree, union-find algorithm, centroids, clustering based on connectivity)
Formal languages and complexity
Automata theory, formal languages, complexity theory, computability
Formal specification and verification
Binary Decision Diagrams, Linear Temporal Logic & Büchi-Automata, Timed Automata, Model Checking
