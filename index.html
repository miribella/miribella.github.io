Hi there! My name is Miriam. Welcome to my personal homepage.

I am an economics and computer science student, living in Munich and Liverpool. I am interested in topics that combine economics, computer science, and statistics.

About Me

I hold a Bachelor of Science in Economics from the University of Munich (LMU Munich). After attending compulsory modules in statistics and econometrics, I specialised in applied econometrics. I am also aiming for a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science at the LMU Munich, which I expect to obtain in summer 2020. In the academic year 2018/2019 I studied at the University of Liverpool on a scholarship as a visiting student. There I attended advanced bachelor and master courses on Algorithmic Game Theory & Mechanism Design, Optimisation, Data Mining, and Neural Networks. This spring, I will be writing my bachelor's thesis on stackelberg routing in routing games and ε-approximate equilibria.

Outside uni campus, you'll probably see me hanging out in cafes with my friends, following current affairs, or riding my bicycle.
