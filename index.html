About Me

I hold a Bachelor of Science in Economics and a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science, both from the University of Munich (LMU Munich). After attending compulsory modules in statistics and econometrics, I specialised in game theory and applied econometrics. I wrote my bachelor thesis on comparing the complexity of strategies by counting the number of states needed to model the strategies as finite automata. For my studies in Computer Science, I studied at the University of Liverpool on a scholarship as a visiting student 2018/2019. There I attended advanced bachelor and master courses on Algorithmic Game Theory & Mechanism Design, Optimisation, Data Mining, and Neural Networks. My bachelor thesis, Stackelberg routing with fairness considerations, dealt with a new type of Stackelberg routing in which the Stackelberg leader is restricted in the factor she is allowed to put controlled players worse off than selfish players. I investigated how this effects the Price of Anarchy and tried to come up with fair Stackelberg strategies.
